   - KERRBB (Li et al. 2005): standard for fitting observations
   - GRAD, DISKBB variants: zero-torque vs. non-zero torque boundary conditions
   - Self-irradiation effects in spectral synthesis
   - Generalized T profile models accounting for non-standard disks

3. **Relativistic spectral effects:**
   - Doppler beaming and frequency shifts from orbital motion
   - Gravitational redshift (1+z) factor from curved spacetime
   - Light bending and photon trajectory distortion
   - Frame-dragging in Kerr metric
   - Asymmetric brightness (approaching vs. receding disk material)

4. **Color and observational properties:**
   - Wien's displacement law: λ_max ∝ T^(-1)
   - Color temperature vs. effective temperature distinction
   - Color factor f_col = T_col/T_eff (spectral hardening)
   - Electron scattering opacity effects on continuum shape

5. **GRMHD + radiative transfer (modern first-principles):**
   - MHD dynamics coupled with spectral synthesis
   - Two-temperature electron models (electrons vs. ions)
   - Ray-tracing integration of local spectra over disk
   - Accretion-state-dependent spectral evolution

6. **Visualization & rendering methodology:**
   - Interstellar/DNEG case study (James et al. 2015): pedagogical approach to Doppler + GR shifts
   - Luminet (1979): first visualization; Doppler/Einstein effect precedent
   - Riazuelo ray-tracing: comprehensive GR implementation
