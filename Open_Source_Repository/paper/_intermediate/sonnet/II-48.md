# Novikov & Thorne (1973) — Sonnet Intermediate Summary

## 1. Identification
- **Authors:** Igor D. Novikov & Kip S. Thorne
- **Year:** 1973
- **Title:** "Astrophysics of Black Holes"
- **Venue:** *Black Holes* (Les Houches 1972 lectures), ed. C. DeWitt & B. DeWitt, Gordon & Breach, 1973.
- The canonical primary source for the **relativistic thin accretion-disk** model (the "Novikov–Thorne disk").

---

## 2. Core Contribution
The paper establishes the general-relativistic theory of a geometrically thin, optically thick accretion disk around a Kerr black hole. It derives the **time-averaged radial structure** (flux, effective temperature, surface density, half-thickness) from conservation of rest mass, energy, and angular momentum, closed by a **zero-torque boundary condition at the ISCO**. The result is largely independent of the unknown viscosity microphysics, giving an explicit, model-independent radiative flux profile $F(r)$ — the foundation for all subsequent black-hole disk spectra and images. Extensive preliminaries (radiative processes, relativistic fluid dynamics, radiative transfer) supply the physics that the disk model assembles.

---

## 3. Key Equations & Quantities
- **Disk efficiency vs spin** [Directly Usable]: radiative efficiency $\eta = L/\dot{M}c^2$ ranges from $\approx 0.057$ (Schwarzschild) to $\approx 0.42$ (extremal Kerr); set by the binding energy at the ISCO, which the spin $a$ controls.
- **Effective temperature (blackbody surface)** [Directly Usable]: $F = \sigma T_{\rm eff}^4$, with the Newtonian/relativistic profile
  $$T(r) \propto \left(\frac{\dot{M} M}{r^3}\right)^{1/4}\left[1-\left(\tfrac{r_{\rm in}}{r}\right)^{1/2}\right]^{1/4}.$$
  At fixed $r/M$: $T \propto \dot{M}^{1/4} M^{-1/2}$.
- **Radiant flux** [Directly Usable]: $F(r) \propto \dot{M}\,M^{-2}\,Q(r)$, where the relativistic correction factor $Q(r)\to 0$ smoothly at the ISCO (eq. 5.6.12) — the inner edge does not radiate.
- **Mass conservation** [Directly Usable]: $\dot{M}_0 = -2\pi\sqrt{-g}\,\Sigma\,u^r$ (steady, axisymmetric).
- **Middle-region temperature law** [Needs adaptation]: $T_s \propto r^{-17/9}$ (gas pressure + electron scattering) — the dominant visual emission band.
- **Vertical structure** [Needs adaptation]: hydrostatic balance $P=\tfrac12\rho_0 g_z h$, viscosity $\nu=\alpha c_s h$, dissipation $Q^+=\tfrac32\alpha P\Omega h$, surface density $\Sigma=2\rho_0 h$.
- **Opacity** [Directly Usable]: electron-scattering $\kappa_{\rm es}=0.40\ \mathrm{cm^2\,g^{-1}}\,f_e$; free–free at lower $T$.
- **Radiative-transfer invariant** [Directly Usable]: $I_\nu/\nu^3$ conserved along photon geodesics (Liouville); $I_\nu=B_\nu(T)$ in the optically thick limit.

---

## 4. Methods / Algorithms
1. **Conservation laws** for rest mass, energy, and angular momentum integrated over a cylindrical control volume of the disk (Gauss's theorem), assuming near-Keplerian equatorial circular geodesics.
2. **Zero-torque ISCO boundary condition:** viscous stress $\to 0$ at the innermost stable circular orbit, fixing the integration constant so that $F(r_{\rm in})=0$.
3. **Viscosity parametrization** ($\alpha$-prescription): viscous stress both heats the gas and transports angular momentum outward; the explicit $\alpha$ value drops out of $F(r)$.
4. **Separated radial/vertical structure:** vertical hydrostatic + radiative-diffusion equations give $T_{\rm eff}$ from $F$; three radial regions (outer gas/free-free, middle gas/electron-scattering, inner radiation/electron-scattering) solved as power laws.

---

## 5. Results & Key Parameters
- **Flux profile:** $F(r)$ rises from zero at the ISCO to a sharp peak just outside it, then decays $\sim r^{-3}$ at large radius.
- **Peak emission** near $r/M \sim 10$; peak temperature $T_{\rm peak}\propto \dot{M}^{1/4}M^{-1/2}$ — soft X-ray ($\sim10^7$ K) for stellar-mass holes, UV/optical for supermassive holes.
- **Spin dependence:** $a$ sets $r_{\rm ISCO}$ (hence the inner edge), the efficiency $\eta$, and the steepness of the temperature gradient. For near-extremal $a=0.999$ the ISCO is close to the horizon, concentrating emission deep in the potential well and maximizing relativistic effects.
- **Validity:** thin-disk approximation $h\ll r$ ($h\sim10^2$–$10^5$ cm); steady, axisymmetric; breaks down at/inside the ISCO and for supercritical $\dot{M}>\dot{M}_{\rm Edd}$ (luminosity self-regulates at $L_{\rm Edd}$).

## 6. Relevance to a Kerr + Accretion-Disk Offline GPU Renderer
- **Disk shading source of truth:** when a traced geodesic hits the equatorial plane at $r\ge r_{\rm ISCO}$, evaluate $F(r)$ → $T_{\rm eff}(r)=(F/\sigma)^{1/4}$ → Planck $B_\nu(T_{\rm eff})$. Inside the ISCO, emission $=0$ (the non-radiating plunge region) — the zero-torque BC is what gives the disk its sharp inner edge.
- **Precompute as a 1D LUT:** $F(r)$ / $T(r)$ depend only on $r$ and fixed $a=0.999$; bake a radius-indexed table rather than integrating per-pixel.
- **Color/SED per ring:** the middle-region $T\propto r^{-17/9}\approx r^{-1.89}$ slope justifies SKILL.md **Decision B** ($T=T_0(6/r)^{0.75}$) as an acceptable rendering approximation.
- **Relativistic transport:** scale local intensity by the redshift/Doppler factor via the invariant $I_\nu/\nu^3$; observed $I_{\nu,\rm obs}=g^3 B_\nu(T_{\rm eff})$, with the disk 4-velocity $u^\mu=u^t(1,0,0,\Omega)$ (Keplerian $\Omega$).
- **Geometry:** half-thickness $h(r)\ll r$ confirms the equatorial-plane (zero-thickness) disk model is adequate; vertical structure can be ignored for first-order imaging.
