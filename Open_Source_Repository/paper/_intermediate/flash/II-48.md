# Technical Analysis: "Astrophysics of Black Holes" (Novikov & Thorne, 1973)

---

### 1. Identification
*   **Authors:** Igor D. Novikov (Institute of Applied Mathematics, Academy of Sciences, Moscow) and Kip S. Thorne (California Institute of Technology, Pasadena).
*   **Year:** 1973
*   **Title:** Astrophysics of Black Holes
*   **Venue:** Chapter 5 of *Black Holes* (Les Houches 1973 Lectures), edited by C. DeWitt and B. DeWitt.

---

### 2. Core Contribution
The paper delivers the foundational general relativistic framework for a geometrically thin, optically thick accretion disk surrounding a rotating (Kerr) black hole (the **Novikov-Thorne (NT) model**). By extending the Newtonian Shakura-Sunyaev $\alpha$-disk model to curved spacetime, the authors derived the complete relativistic radial structure, vertical momentum balance, and local energy dissipation profiles. The core contribution is showing that local mass, angular momentum, and energy conservation equations can be integrated analytically in Kerr spacetime up to a single radial integration constant, determined by a vanishing-torque boundary condition at the innermost stable circular orbit (ISCO).

---

### 3. Key Equations & Quantities

#### Coordinate System & Metric
The disk lies in the equatorial plane ($\theta = \pi/2$) of a Kerr black hole of mass $M$ and spin parameter $a$. In Boyer-Lindquist coordinates $(t, r, \theta, \phi)$ with $z = r\cos\theta \approx 0$, the metric is defined by:
$$ds^2 = -\frac{r^2 \Delta}{A} dt^2 + \frac{A}{r^2}(d\phi - \omega dt)^2 + \frac{r^2}{\Delta} dr^2 + dz^2$$
where the auxiliary metric functions are:
*   $\Delta = r^2 - 2Mr + a^2$
*   $A = r^4 + r^2 a^2 + 2M r a^2$
*   $\omega = \frac{2Mar}{A}$ (frame-dragging angular velocity)

#### Relativistic Correction Factors (Dimensionless, $r_* = r/M$, $a_* = a/M$)
These algebraic factors account for general relativistic deviations from Newtonian gravity:
*   $\mathcal{B} = 1 + a_* r_*^{-3/2}$
*   $\mathcal{C} = 1 - 3r_*^{-1} + 2a_* r_*^{-3/2}$
*   $\mathcal{D} = 1 - 2r_*^{-1} + a_*^2 r_*^{-2}$
*   $\mathcal{F} = 1 - 2a_* r_*^{-3/2} + a_*^2 r_*^{-2}$
*   $\mathcal{G} = 1 - 2r_*^{-1} + a_* r_*^{-3/2}$

#### Circular Geodesic Orbits (Directly Usable in Renderer)
The Keplerian angular velocity $\Omega$, specific energy $\tilde{E}$, and specific angular momentum $\tilde{L}$ of the disk material in the equatorial plane are:
$$\Omega = \frac{d\phi}{dt} = \frac{M^{1/2}}{r^{3/2} + a M^{1/2}} = \frac{1}{M(r_*^{3/2} + a_*)}$$
$$\tilde{E} = \frac{\mathcal{G}}{\mathcal{C}^{1/2}}$$
$$\tilde{L} = M r_*^{1/2} \frac{\mathcal{F}}{\mathcal{C}^{1/2}}$$

#### The Innermost Stable Circular Orbit (ISCO, $r_{ms}$)
Determines the sharp inner cutoff of the radiating disk:
$$r_{ms} = M \left\{ 3 + Z_2 - \left[ (3 - Z_1)(3 + Z_1 + 2 Z_2) \right]^{1/2} \right\}$$
$$Z_1 = 1 + (1 - a_*^2)^{1/3} \left[ (1 + a_*)^{1/3} + (1 - a_*)^{1/3} \right]$$
$$Z_2 = (3 a_*^2 + Z_1^2)^{1/2}$$

#### Emitted Radiative Flux $F(r)$ (Directly Usable in Renderer)
The flux emitted from one surface of the disk at radius $r$ is:
$$F(r) = \frac{3 M \dot{M}_0}{8 \pi r^3} \frac{\mathcal{Q}(r)}{\mathcal{B} \mathcal{C}^{1/2} \mathcal{D}^{1/2}}$$
where $\dot{M}_0$ is the constant accretion rate, and $\mathcal{Q}(r)$ is the dimensionless radial integral:
$$\mathcal{Q}(r) = \mathcal{B} \mathcal{C}^{1/2} \int_{r_{ms}}^r \frac{\tilde{E} \frac{d\tilde{L}}{dr} - \tilde{L} \frac{d\tilde{E}}{dr}}{\mathcal{B} \mathcal{C}^2 \mathcal{D}^{1/2}} dr$$

### 4. Methods/Algorithms

#### Height-Integrated Conservation Laws
The 3D hydrodynamic transport is vertically integrated over the half-thickness $h(r)$ of the disk. The local density $\rho_0$, shear stress $t_{\hat{\phi}\hat{r}}$, and vertical radiative flux $q^z$ are mapped to surface-integrated fields:
*   $\Sigma(r) = \int_{-h}^h \langle \rho_0 \rangle dz$ (Surface density)
*   $W(r) = \int_{-h}^h \langle t_{\hat{\phi}\hat{r}} \rangle dz$ (Integrated shear stress)
*   $F(r) = \langle q^z \rangle$ (Surface flux)

#### Boundary Conditions and Plunge Regime
The model assumes the disk is in a steady state ($\partial/\partial t = 0$) and axisymmetric ($\partial/\partial \phi = 0$). The crucial inner boundary condition is that the viscous torque vanishes at the ISCO:
$$W(r_{ms}) = 0$$
Gas inside $r < r_{ms}$ is assumed to be in dynamic, non-interacting plunge along geodesic trajectories, meaning its contribution to the disk's radiation emission is neglected in the standard model.

---

### 5. Results & Key Parameters
*   **Spin dependency:** For a near-extremal black hole ($a_* = 0.999$), $r_{ms}$ moves down to $\approx 1.18 M$, compared to $6 M$ for a Schwarzschild black hole ($a_* = 0$). This increases the accretion efficiency $\eta = 1 - \tilde{E}(r_{ms})$ from $5.7\%$ up to $\approx 30\%$.
*   **Disk Structure Regimes:** The disk divides into three radial zones based on dominant pressure and opacity:
    1.  *Outer Region:* Gas pressure dominates; free-free opacity ($\kappa_{ff} \propto \rho_0 T^{-7/2}$) dominates.
    2.  *Middle Region:* Gas pressure dominates; electron scattering ($\kappa_{es} = 0.40 \text{ cm}^2/\text{g}$) dominates.
    3.  *Inner Region:* Radiation pressure dominates; electron scattering ($\kappa_{es}$) dominates.

### 6. Relevance to a Kerr + Accretion-Disk Offline Renderer

An offline GPU renderer utilizing geodesic ray tracing must implement the following physical mappings directly from this paper:

#### Disk Geometry & Intersection
*   Represent the disk as a flat, infinitely thin sheet located at the equatorial plane ($\theta = \pi/2$).
*   The valid domain for intersection testing and color evaluation is $r_{ms} \le r \le r_{out}$.
*   Set the inner boundary $r_{ms}$ using the analytical $Z_1, Z_2$ equations for $a = 0.999$.

#### Local Emission Evaluation
*   Precompute the radial profile of the flux $F(r)$ (or the dimensionless function $f_{NT}(r) = \frac{\mathcal{Q}(r)}{\mathcal{B} \mathcal{C}^{1/2} \mathcal{D}^{1/2}}$) on the CPU as a 1D lookup table (LUT) to avoid expensive numerical integration of $\mathcal{Q}(r)$ inside the GPU intersection shader.
*   The local effective blackbody temperature is $T_{eff}(r) = \left( F(r)/\sigma_{SB} \right)^{1/4}$.
*   Evaluate local intensity using Planck's law:
    $$I_{\nu, emit}(r) = \frac{2 h_P \nu^3}{c^2 \left( \exp\left(\frac{h_P \nu}{k_B T_{eff}(r)}\right) - 1 \right)}$$

#### Relativistic Ray-Tracing Corrections
To compute the observed intensity of a ray hitting the disk, the renderer must compute the local four-velocity of the disk material:
$$u^\mu_{\text{disk}} = u^t \left( 1, 0, 0, \Omega \right)$$
$$u^t = \left( -g_{tt} - 2\Omega g_{t\phi} - \Omega^2 g_{\phi\phi} \right)^{-1/2}$$
Using the photon's four-momentum $p_\mu$ at the intersection point, the redshift factor $g$ is:
$$g = \frac{\nu_{\text{obs}}}{\nu_{\text{emit}}} = \frac{p_\mu u^\mu_{\text{observer}}}{p_\mu u^\mu_{\text{disk}}}$$
For an observer at rest at infinity ($u^\mu_{\text{observer}} = (1, 0, 0, 0)$):
$$g = \frac{1}{u^t \left( 1 - \Omega b \right)}$$
where $b = -p_\phi / p_t$ is the constant of motion (impact parameter) of the ray.

The final rendered pixel intensity is scaled using Liouville’s invariant:
$$I_{\nu, obs} = g^3 I_{\nu, emit}$$
This correctly accounts for both gravitational redshift and relativistic Doppler beaming near the $a=0.999$ horizon.
