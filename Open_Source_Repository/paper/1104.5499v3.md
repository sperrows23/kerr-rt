# Page-Thorne (1974) Accretion Disk Flux Model
**Source:** Derived from Page & Thorne (1974) ApJ 191, 499–506 and Abramowicz & Fragile (2013) Living Rev. Relativity.

## 1. The Flux Integral
The time-averaged radiative energy flux $F(r)$ emerging from the surface of an accretion disk is derived from the conservation of energy and angular momentum. 

Let $E$ be the specific orbital energy, $L$ the specific angular momentum, and $\Omega$ the angular velocity. The flux integral is:

$$ F(r) = - \frac{\dot{M}}{4\pi \sqrt{-g}} \frac{(d\Omega/dr)}{(E - \Omega L)^2} \int_{r_{ms}}^r (E - \Omega L) \frac{dL}{dr} dr $$

Using the fundamental thermodynamic relation for Keplerian circular orbits ($dE/dr = \Omega \, dL/dr$), the integrand $(E - \Omega L)(dL/dr)$ can be rewritten to explicitly show the numerator term $(E L' - L E')$. The alternative formulation is:

$$ F(r) = - \frac{\dot{M}}{4\pi \sqrt{-g}} \frac{(d\Omega/dr)}{(E - \Omega L)^2} \int_{r_{ms}}^r \left( E \frac{dL}{dr} - L \frac{dE}{dr} \right) dr $$

## 2. The Zero-Torque Inner Boundary Condition
The model assumes that internal viscous torques vanish at the Innermost Stable Circular Orbit (ISCO), denoted as $r_{ms}$. By setting the lower limit of the integration to $r_{ms}$, the integral evaluates to zero at the boundary, yielding the mathematical condition:

$$ F(r_{ms}) = 0 $$

## 3. The Closed-Form Page-Thorne Flux (Eq. 15n)
Integrating the flux expression within the Kerr metric yields a closed-form algebraic representation. Using the relativistic correction functions, the emergent flux scales as:

$$ F(r) \propto r_*^{-3} \mathcal{B}^{-1} \mathcal{C}^{-1/2} \mathcal{Q} $$

Where the radial coordinate is dimensionless $y = \sqrt{r/M}$, the dimensionless black hole spin is $a_* = a/M$, and the scaled radius is $r_* = r c^2 / GM$. The primary integral function $\mathcal{Q}$ is defined as:

$$ 
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal{Q} = \mathcal{Q}_0 \Bigg[ & y - y_0 - \frac{3}{2} a_* \ln \left( \frac{y}{y_0} \right) \\
& - \frac{3(y_1 - a_*)^2}{y_1(y_1 - y_2)(y_1 - y_3)} \ln \left( \frac{y - y_1}{y_0 - y_1} \right) \\
& - \frac{3(y_2 - a_*)^2}{y_2(y_2 - y_1)(y_2 - y_3)} \ln \left( \frac{y - y_2}{y_0 - y_2} \right) \\
& - \frac{3(y_3 - a_*)^2}{y_3(y_3 - y_1)(y_3 - y_2)} \ln \left( \frac{y - y_3}{y_0 - y_3} \right) \Bigg]
\end{aligned}
$$

### Relativistic Correction Functions
The supplementary relativistic functions required to evaluate the flux are:

$$ \mathcal{B} = 1 + a_* y^{-3} $$

$$ \mathcal{C} = 1 - 3y^{-2} + 2a_* y^{-3} $$

$$ \mathcal{Q}_0 = \frac{1 + a_* y^{-3}}{y(1 - 3y^{-2} + 2a_*y^{-3})^{1/2}} $$

### Roots for the Integration
The constants $y_0, y_1, y_2, y_3$ are derived from the ISCO boundary and the roots of the cubic equation $y^3 - 3y + 2a_* = 0$. 

The lower integration limit corresponding to the ISCO is:
$$ y_0 = \sqrt{\frac{r_{ms}}{M}} $$

The three roots of the cubic equation are:
$$ y_1 = 2 \cos\left[ \frac{\cos^{-1}(a_*) - \pi}{3} \right] $$

$$ y_2 = 2 \cos\left[ \frac{\cos^{-1}(a_*) + \pi}{3} \right] $$

$$ y_3 = -2 \cos\left[ \frac{\cos^{-1}(a_*)}{3} \right] $$
